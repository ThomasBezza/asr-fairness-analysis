## 7. Key Takeaways

- **No multicollinearity.** All VIFs in Model 2 are below 1.25, meaning the resource-level and reference-condition predictors are effectively orthogonal in this sample. Standard errors and p-values are not inflated by collinearity.

- **Homoscedasticity holds.** Breusch-Pagan LM tests are non-significant for all three models (p > 0.33), and the residual plots show no fan-shaped patterns. The OLS assumption of constant error variance is not violated.

- **One influential observation (OBS034) does not drive the null result.** Whisper large-v2 on the AMI benchmark has an unusually high WER (36.4%) and a Cook's distance of 0.192. However, excluding it leaves model p-values and R² virtually unchanged, confirming the null finding is robust.

- **Right-skewed residuals are a genuine but tolerable limitation.** The Q-Q plots consistently show lighter-than-normal left tails and one-to-two extreme upper-tail observations. With n = 50 and only categorical predictors, moderate non-normality of residuals does not invalidate the inference under the central limit theorem, but it does mean that WER data in this sample are not well-approximated by a normal distribution — a point worth acknowledging in the paper's limitations section.

- **Neither added term improves model fit.** The LR tests confirm that including reference condition (M1 → M2, p = 0.980) and the resource × reference interaction (M2 → M3, p = 0.314) both fail to significantly improve the model. Taken together with the non-significant coefficients in all three models, the diagnostics support the original Model 2 conclusion: there is no statistically significant association between resource level or reference condition and reported WER in this sample.
